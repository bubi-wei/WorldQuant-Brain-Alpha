# Recommended Alpha Research Papers

This note groups recommended papers for the WorldQuant Brain Alpha project.

## Price and Volume (pv1)

1. Jegadeesh, N. and Titman, S. (1993). Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers.
   - Classic momentum evidence in cross-section.
2. Chordia, T., Roll, R., and Subrahmanyam, A. (2001). Market Liquidity and Trading Activity.
   - Connects liquidity, volume, and expected returns.
3. Ang, A. et al. (2006). The Cross-Section of Volatility and Expected Returns.
   - Low-volatility anomaly and risk pricing implications.
4. Avramov, D. and Chordia, T. (2006). Predicting Stock Returns.
   - Uses predictors including technical variables and volume-related signals.

## Fundamentals (fundamental6)

1. Fama, E. and French, K. (2015). A Five-Factor Asset Pricing Model.
   - Profitability and investment factors as robust return drivers.
2. Novy-Marx, R. (2013). The Other Side of Value: The Gross Profitability Premium.
   - Profitability as a strong cross-sectional signal.
3. Sloan, R. (1996). Do Stock Prices Fully Reflect Information in Accruals.
   - Accrual anomaly and earnings quality.
4. Piotroski, J. (2000). Value Investing: The Use of Historical Financial Statement Information.
   - Composite accounting signals for stock selection.

## Analyst and Estimates (analyst4)

1. Chan, L., Jegadeesh, N., and Lakonishok, J. (1996). Momentum Strategies.
   - Includes evidence around earnings-related information diffusion.
2. Dreman, D. and Berry, M. (1995). Analyst Forecasting Errors and Their Implications.
   - Forecast bias and subsequent return behavior.
3. Bradshaw, M. et al. (2001). Do Analysts and Auditors Use Information in Accruals.
   - Links analyst behavior with accounting information quality.

## General and ML

1. Gu, S., Kelly, B., and Xiu, D. (2020). Empirical Asset Pricing via Machine Learning.
   - Nonlinear factor discovery and predictor interactions.
2. Harvey, C., Liu, Y., and Zhu, H. (2016). ... and the Cross-Section of Expected Returns.
   - Multiple-testing caution for factor mining.
